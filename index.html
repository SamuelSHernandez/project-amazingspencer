Coronavirus

Topic Overview

Around February 2020, rumors began to spread of a certain virus that could have devastading consequences. In March 2020, the coronavirus made its debut and had spread to the United States. The coronavirus causes symptoms of shortness of breath or difficulty breathing, fever or chills, cough, fatigue, muscle or body aches, headache, new loss of taste or smell, sore throat, congestion or runny nose, nausea or vomiting, and diarrhea. Symptoms listed here are from the CDC. In an attempt to slow the spread, the country was shut down for months and preventative measures were put into place to slow the spread. Now after being shut down, the country is beginning to reopen and businesses are opening their doors with preventative measures still in place. These preventive measures include wearing face masks and social distancing. As Americans are getting back into the office and perhaps going to the grocery store for the first time in months, Americans need to know the extent of this virus and what the stats of the coronavirus are.

Currently, the CDC recommends that you wash your hands often, avoid close contact, cover your mouth and nose with a mask when around others, cover coughs and sneezes, clean and disinfect, and monitor your health daily to prevent contracting the coronavirus.

John Hopkins University provides a global map of coronavirus hotspots and known locations. This site also includes number of cases, number of deaths, and trends for the virus.

I believe that there is a real need for web applications for the coronavirus. People want to be able to visit a site that will inform and provide updated statistics on the extent of the coronavirus. People want to be able to quickly and easily locate information related to the coronavirus. People will also want to see how their local governments are handling this virus and the web app could provide links to local government sites.

Potential Applications

  • Coronavirus Information Site
  • Disease Information Site
  • Forget-the-Coronavirus Site

Coronavirus Information Site

The Coronavirus Information Site will include statistical data about the number of cases, number of deaths, geographical information such as maps that show hotspots, as well as articles from reliable sources on the coronavirus. To use the app, all it takes is visiting the site and exploring the content and information available at your fingertips. Users will be able to navigate to a map graphic, access articles, and be able to see coronavirus stats provided by reliable sources such as the CDC.

Disease Information Site

The Disease Information Site will be similar in style to the Coronavirus Information Site with the primary difference being that this site will be more broad in scope. This site will have a listing for major diseases in the United States and pertinent facts and information regarding each of those diseases. The site will also include map data and graphics pertaining to each disease listed.

Forget-the-Coronavirus Site

The Forget-the-Coronavirus Site is a site that will be a place to get your mind off of the coronavirus and all of the world's problems. Sometimes we as humans focus so much on the negative of what is happening around us that sometimes we need to take a break and focus on the positive. To accomplish that, this site will provide uplifting articles for reading, interactive games and puzzles, and links to other positive sites. Users will be able to visit this site to take their mind off of the negative and focus on the positive.
